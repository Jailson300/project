CULTURE AND TRADITION

DANCE :

The Meitei tribe of Manipur is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, especially its vibrant and diverse dance traditions. These dances are deeply intertwined with the community's religious, social, and cultural practices. Here are some of the most significant cultural dances of the Meitei tribe:

Khamba Thoibi is a duet dance performed by a male and female dancer, depicting the legendary love story of Khamba and Thoibi from the Moirang region.The dance is characterized by its elegant and expressive movements, symbolizing love and devotion.

Ras Lila is the classical dance of Manipur, depicting the love story of Radha and Krishna. It is performed during festivals and religious occasions.The dance is characterized by its grace, fluid movements, and expressions of devotion. Performers wear traditional costumes, including elaborate jewelry and beautifully designed skirts (Lai-Ningthou).

Also known as the Drum Dance, Pung Cholom is a highly energetic and rhythmic dance form. Dancers perform acrobatic feats and complex rhythmic patterns while playing the Pung (drum). It requires great skill, strength, and stamina.The primary instrument is the Pung, and the dance often includes vocal accompaniment

Nupi Jagoi is one of the many dances performed during the Lai Haraoba festival. It is performed by women and is characterized by its graceful and expressive movements.The dance is a vital part of the Lai Haraoba rituals, representing the divine and the creation of life. It is a form of worship and invocation of the deities.Dancers wear traditional attire, including the Phanek (a wrap-around skirt), Innaphi (a shawl or upper garment).

MUSIC :

The Meitei tribe of Manipur has a rich musical heritage that plays an integral role in their cultural, religious, and social life. Their music is characterized by a unique blend of traditional instruments, vocal styles, and rhythmic patterns, often deeply connected to their rituals, dances, and festivals. Here are some of the prominent aspects of Meitei music:

VOCAL MUSIC :

Khunung-esei,traditional folk songs that narrate stories of everyday life, love, nature, and historical events.Often sung during community gatherings, festivals, and agricultural activities.These songs preserve the oral history and cultural heritage of the Meitei people.

Nat-Sankirtana is a form of devotional music that combines singing, dancing, and playing instruments like the Pung and Kartal.Performed during religious ceremonies, particularly in temples and at festivals like Janmashtami.Nat Sankirtana is a spiritual practice, bringing the community together in devotion and worship.

Pena-Ashangba is a form of music performed by Pena singers, often narrating mythological stories and historical events.Accompanying rituals, storytelling sessions, and festivals.ena Ashangba is a vital tradition, preserving the cultural and historical narratives of the Meitei people.

TRADITIONAL INSTRUMENT :

The Pena is a traditional string instrument made from bamboo and a gourd, often played with a bow.It is used in both folk and classical music, particularly in rituals and dances like Lai Haraoba and Pena singing.The Pena is considered a sacred instrument, often associated with storytelling and spiritual ceremonies.

The Pung is a traditional drum, often referred to as the "Mridanga" of Manipur.It is the primary percussion instrument in Manipuri dance forms such as Pung Cholom (Drum Dance) and Ras Lila.The Pung is central to many Meitei musical performances, providing rhythmic accompaniment and driving the tempo of the dance.

Dhol is a traditional drums used in various musical performances.Commonly used in folk music and during festivals.These drums add to the percussive depth of Meitei music.

Bansi is a type of flute made from bamboo.Used in both solo and ensemble performances, often accompanying dances and songs.The Bansi provides melodic lines and is essential in creating the distinctive sound of Meitei music.

TRADITIONAL DRESS :

Phanek is a traditional wrap-around skirt worn by Meitei women. It is typically ankle-length and made from handwoven fabric.This type of Phanek is known as Phanek-Mayek-Naibi as it features horizontal stripes or patterns (Mayek) that symbolize different elements of nature.

Inaphi is a traditional shawl or upper garment worn over the Phanek. It is draped around the upper body, covering the shoulders.It is often made from fine cotton or silk, it may be plain or decorated with embroidery and intricate patterns.

Here it shows three different dress : LENGYAN , KURTA and PHEIJOM.

The LENGYAN is a traditional shawl for the Meitei man.Its often worn over Kurta with Pheijom over his lower body.Its mostly wear during important functions such as Wedding.

KURTA is a traditional long tunic worn over the Dhoti. It is usually made from cotton or silk and may feature simple or intricate designs.The Kurta can be plain or embroidered, and it typically reaches the knees.It is worn during festivals, ceremonies, and formal occasions.

PHEIJOM is a traditional garment worn by Meitei men. It is a rectangular piece of cloth wrapped around the waist and legs.It is made from Handwoven cotton or silk,it is white with borders of different color and pattern.It is mostly during festivals,religious ceremonies and cultural events.

KHUDEI is a traditional garment worn by Meitei men. It is also a rectangular piece of cloth wrapped around the waist and legs.It is a handwoven cotton cloth with stripes vertically and horizontally forming a small square type design all over the cloth.It has border with different color and different pattern.It is found in different types of color as well.It is mostly use daily and for some religious events and cultural events.

This is a traditional Meitei-Groom outfit, It is a combination of a plain LENGYAN , PHEIJOM and a plain KURTA.The Groom also wears a turban referred to as "Kok-yet".

This a traditional Meitei-Bride outfit,mostly worn by bride from the follower of Sanamahism.It is a combinational outfit of a small Innaphi , Phanek-Mayek-Naibi and some accessories.The Bride also wears a headgear known as "Kajenglei".

The Potloi is an elaborate cylindrical skirt worn by the bride from Meitei-Hindu family. It is one of the most distinctive elements of the Lenyan.Potloi is heavily embellished with mirrors, sequins, beads, and intricate embroidery. It is designed to be eye-catching and regal, reflecting the importance of the occasion.

Khamba is a legendary hero in the epic tale of Khamba Thoibi, a famous love story from the Meitei folklore of Manipur.Khamba's dress is a representation of his heroic status and cultural identity. Each element of the attire holds symbolic meaning, reflecting the traditional values and heritage of the Meitei community.Khamba's dress is often used in cultural performances, dance dramas, and storytelling sessions, helping to preserve and celebrate the rich folklore and traditions of the Meitei people.

Thoibi is a prominent figure in Manipuri folklore, particularly in the epic love story of Khamba Thoibi. Thoibi's traditional dress is a beautifully crafted ensemble that embodies the elegance, nobility, and cultural heritage of the Meitei tribe. From the intricately decorated Phanek and Innaphi to the elaborate Potloi and traditional ornaments, each component of Thoibi's attire is designed to honor and celebrate her legendary status. This traditional attire plays a crucial role in cultural representations, symbolizing not only Thoibi's personal elegance but also her connection to the cultural roots of the Meitei community.
