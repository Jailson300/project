HISTORICAL SITES

KANGLA

The Kangla, officially known as the Kangla Fort, is an old fortified palace at Imphal, in the Manipur state of India. It was formerly situated on both sides of the bank of the Imphal River, now remaining only on the western side in ruined conditions. Kangla means "the prominent part of the dry land" in old Meetei.Kangla Palace is one of Manipur's oldest and most historically significant buildings.

KHONGJOM-WAR-MEMORIAL

Khongjom War Memorial Complex is a war memorial commemorating the Anglo-Manipur War. Situated at Khongjom, Thoubal district of Manipur, the complex houses the world's tallest sword statue.The Battle of Khongjom was possibly the greatest war the Manipuris fought against the British and the Khongjom War Memorial Complex has been built in commemoration of the brave Manipuri soldiers who lost their lives in the war. This historical attraction is located at a distance of about 33 km from Imphal and draws all history lovers to it as it relays the grueling fight fought in 1891.Khongjom Day is celebrated every year on 23rd April in remembrance of the brave soldiers.

PAKHANGBA TEMPLE

The Pakhangba Temple, also known as the Pakhangba Laishang , is a Meitei temple dedicated to the God Pakhangba of Sanamahism, the traditional Meitei religion, located beside the Nungseng Eekon, to the left side of the Kangla Sanathong, the western gate of the Kangla Fort in the Imphal West district of Manipur.

IMA KEITHEL(Mother's Market)

Ima Keithel, also known as Nupi Keithel, is one of the largest all-women markets in Asia. It has been a significant commercial and cultural hub for centuries.Ima Keithel has a long history, dating back to ancient times when it served as a marketplace for local women to trade goods and produce.ma Keithel is a vibrant cultural site where traditional crafts, textiles, and foods are sold, reflecting the rich cultural heritage of the Meitei people.The market is run entirely by women, highlighting the important role of women in Meitei society and economy.It was established in the 16th century and hosts around 5,000-6,000 women vendors who sell a variety of products.
