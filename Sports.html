Indigenous Sports

Mukna(Manipuri Wrestling)

Mukna is a traditional form of wrestling that has been practiced in Manipur for centuries. It involves grappling techniques and is often performed during festivals and important cultural events. Wrestlers aim to throw their opponent to the ground using skillful techniques.

Sagol-kangjei(Polo)

Sagol Kangjei is believed to be one of the origins of modern polo. It is played with seven players on each team, riding Manipuri ponies. The game is fast-paced and requires a high level of skill in both riding and hitting the ball with a long-handled mallet.

Arambai Hunba (Manipuri Dart Throwing)

This traditional sport involves the skillful throwing of arambai, which are dart-like weapons. It was historically used by Manipuri warriors. The sport requires precision and control.Arambai, literally meaning darts, is a simple weapon at first sight. The weapon has an iron arrow head, which is attached to feathers of peacocks though kites' feathers were also used in the past. Users of these weapons, who are horse riders, swirl the deadly poisoned darts and throw it to the enemy with an unparalleled accuracy and speed which is gathered through fast galloping of the Manipuri ponies.

Yubi Lakpi (Manipuri Rugby)

Yubi Lakpi, which translates to "coconut snatching," is a game similar to rugby but played with a coconut instead of a ball. The objective is to carry the coconut and touch the opponent's goal line. The game is played barefoot, and it emphasizes agility and strength.

Thang Ta (Manipuri Martial Arts)

Thang Ta is a traditional martial art form that includes both armed and unarmed combat techniques. It involves the use of swords (thang) and spears (ta), as well as hand-to-hand combat techniques. Thang Ta is an integral part of the martial culture of the Meitei people.This aspect of Thang Ta involves unarmed combat techniques such as strikes, kicks, blocks, and grappling. It focuses on agility, speed, and the effective use of one's body to overcome opponents.Thang Ta includes various forms or sequences of movements (called "sajol"). These forms are practiced repeatedly to develop muscle memory and precision.

Hiyang Tannaba (Boat Racing)

Hiyang Tannaba is a traditional boat race held during the Lai Haraoba festival. The boats, known as hiyang, are beautifully decorated and rowed by a team of oarsmen. The race is a display of strength, coordination, and cultural heritage.

SOME OF MEITEI SPORT PERSONS

There are various sports person from the meitei community who have contributed in the sports industry for Manipur and India in the National and International Platforms. Here are some of the sports person who have excelled in their respective field's :

Woman Section:

Nameirakpam Kunjarani Devi

Weightlifter

Nameirakpam Kunjarani Devi was born 1 March, 1968. She is a noted weightlifter from the state of Manipur. She received the Arjuna award in 1990 and also shared the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna award with Leander Paes for the year 1996-1997. She also won the K.K.Birla Sports award in the same year.

Saikhom Mirabai Chanu

Weightlifter

Saikhom Mirabai Chanu was born on 8 August 1994. Mirabai Chanu is an Indian weightlifter who won silver medal in the women's 48 kg weight class at Commonwealth Games, 2014 at Glasgow.Mirabai Chanu lifted a total of 201 kg, winning the gold medal at the CWG 2022. She won the silver medal at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics in the Women's 49 kg category.

Khumukcham Sanjita Chanu

Weightlifter

Khumukcham Sanjita Chanu was born on 2nd January 1994. She is an Indian weightlifter who won gold medal in the women's 48 kg weight class at the Commonwealth Games , 2014 at Glasgow

Laishram Sarita Devi

Indian professional boxer

Laishram Sarita Devi is an Indian boxer from Manipur. She is a national champion and a former world champion in the lightweight class. In 2009, she was awarded Arjuna award by the government of India for her achievement

Samjetsabam Sarjubala Devi

Indian professional boxer

Samjetsabam Sarjubala Devi is an Indian woman boxer from Manipur and represented India at the 2016 Rio Olympics. After being awarded as the Best Boxer at Youth World Women Boxing Championship organised at Turkey, the Olympic Gold Quest announced support for Sarjubala Devi in 2012.

Pukhrambam Sushila Chanu

Hockey Player

Sushila Chanu Pukhrambam is an Indian field hockey player. A former captain of Indian national team, she has over 150 international caps to her credit. Born in Imphal, Manipur, Chanu began playing hockey at the age of eleven, and was soon selected for the national camp. She was awarded Arjuna Award for year 2023.

Sanggai Ibemhal Chanu Maimom

Hockey player

Sanggai Ibemhal Chanu Maimom is a female field hockey player from India, who made her international debut for her native country in April 1998 in a friendly against Germany. She played as a midfielder or as a centre forward. At the 2001 World Cup Qualifier she was named Young Player of the Tournament.

Kharibam Bichu Devi

Hockey Player

Bichu Devi Kharibam is a field hockey player and member of Indian women's hockey team. She hails from Manipur. She plays for Indian Oil Corporation Limited in the domestic hockey tournaments. She is a goalkeeper.

Elangbam Panthoi Chanu

Indian footballer

Elangbam Panthoi Chanu is an Indian professional footballer who plays as a goalkeeper for the South Australia WNPL club Metro United WFC and India women's football team.

Ngangom Bala Devi

Indian footballer

Ngangom Bala Devi (born 2 February 1990) is an Indian professional footballer who plays as a forward for the club Manipur Police and the India national team.

Loitongbam Ashalata Devi

Indian footballer

Loitongbam Ashalata Devi, known as Ashalata Devi, is an Indian professional footballer who plays as a defender for the Indian Women's League club Gokulam Kerala and captains the India women's national team. Devi is considered one of the finest defenders from Asia.

Men's Section

Laishram Devendro Singh

Indian boxer

Devendro Singh Laishram, also known as Devendro Singh or Devendro Laishram, is an Indian boxer from Imphal West district, Manipur who competes in the light-flyweight division.

Ngangom Dingko Singh

Indian Boxer

Ngangom Dingko Singh, commonly known as 'Dingko Singh' was an Indian boxer. He is considered to have been one of the most outstanding boxers India ever produced. He won the King's Cup in Bangkok in 1997 and the Asian Games gold in the 1998 Bangkok Games. He was a service personnel of the Indian Navy.

Thokchom Nanao Singh

Indian Boxer

Konsham Chinglensana Singh

Indian footballer

Moirangthem Dheeraj Singh

Indian footballer

Thounaojam Jeakson Singh

Indian footballer

Jeakson Singh Thounaojam (born 21 June 2001) is an Indian professional footballer who plays as a defensive midfielder for Indian Super League club Kerala Blasters and the India national team.

Khadangbam Kothajit Singh

Indian Hockey Player

Khadangbam Kothajit Singh (born 17 August 1992) is an India field hockey player who plays as a defender or midfielder for the Indian national team.He represented India in Men's Hockey during the 2012 London Olympics. He is the third hockey Olympian, after Pangambam Nilakomol Singh and Ksh.Thoiba from the state of Manipur.

Shanglakpam Nilakanta Sharma

Indian Hockey Player

Shanglakpam Nilakanta Sharma is an Indian field hockey player who plays as a midfielder for the Indian national team. He was part of the Indian squad that won the 2016 Men's Hockey Junior World Cup. And Finally he won gold medal in 2022 Asian Games in Hangzhou.

Kangujam Chinglensana Singh

Indian Hockey Player
